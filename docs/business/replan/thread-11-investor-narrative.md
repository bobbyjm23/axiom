## Investor FAQ

### Q1: "You're solo — who sells?"

**A:** I am primary seller for 12 months — standard for pre-seed B2B. Track A deals close in 4–10 weeks at $12–35k without enterprise procurement. Hire #1 is delivery engineer at 2 signed QuickStarts so I'm not delivery-blocked. Inside sales at seed.

### Q2: "Services businesses don't scale."

**A:** Correct if every deal is bespoke. We productised the QuickStart: same bootstrap, same 3 workspaces, same runbook. Target 68% gross margin on QuickStart, 40% pilot conversion, support ARR stacking. Deploy time is the metric that proves scale — 10 days → 5 → 2.

### Q3: "Cetus is already funded."

**A:** Cetus optimises for enterprise and government with 8–14 week pilots and IRAP. We own mid-market law and accounting where the buyer is a managing partner with $15k discretionary budget. Different ICP, different velocity. We'll move upmarket with references in Year 2.

### Q4: "What if Copilot wins?"

**A:** Copilot wins when US data processing is acceptable and M365 integration is paramount. We target firms where **client confidentiality** prohibits public AI and **Copilot TCO** at 50+ seats triggers budget scrutiny. We're not replacing M365 — we're replacing unapproved ChatGPT.

### Q5: "What's the moat?"

**A:** Runbook + reference density in AU professional services verticals. Technical moat is moderate (open-source stack); **go-to-market moat** is speed, transparent pricing, and desktop UX. First 10 law firm logos create switching cost for competitors entering the niche.
