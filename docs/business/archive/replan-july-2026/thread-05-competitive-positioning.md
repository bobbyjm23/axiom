## Objection Handlers (Investor Q&A)

### 1. "Isn't this a services business, not a venture-scale company?"

**Answer:** Year 1 is services-led by design — that's how you land in regulated markets. The productised QuickStart runbook, ARR from support/managed, and declining deploy time (10d → 5d → 2d) show the path to **repeatable unit economics**. Seed capital scales delivery automation and inside sales, not R&D. Target: 40% pilot→production, $400k ARR, 10+ logos before seed.

### 2. "Cetus and others are already funded — why won't they crush you?"

**Answer:** They're optimised for **enterprise and government** (8–14 week sales, IRAP-heavy). We own **mid-market professional services** (50–250 staff) where decisions happen in 4–10 weeks and $12–35k pilots don't need board approval. Different ICP, different velocity. We don't compete on IRAP Year 1.

### 3. "What stops Microsoft from making Copilot on-prem?"

**Answer:** Microsoft's model is cloud SaaS rental — on-prem sovereignty conflicts with their revenue model. Even if they improve, customers still face **per-seat recurring cost** and US-controlled processing. We offer **CapEx infrastructure the client owns** with open-weight models and no vendor inference lock-in.
