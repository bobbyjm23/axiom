## 1. The problem

Staff already use ChatGPT and Claude on client matters — in browsers, on personal accounts, with no firm approval trail. That is shadow AI: client data leaving the firm without governance, citations, or audit logs. For mid-market professional firms, it is a Privacy Act and professional-duty exposure, not a hypothetical risk.

---

## 2. Why mid-market is stuck

| Blocker | What firms hit |
|---------|----------------|
| **Copilot cost** | Per-seat Microsoft Copilot pricing does not fit 50–250 person firms that need firm-wide adoption, not a pilot for ten partners. |
| **US inference default** | Most “enterprise AI” still routes prompts to US-hosted models by default — a sovereignty and client-confidentiality problem for Australian firms. |
| **No citations / audit** | Generic assistants do not cite firm documents, do not log who asked what, and do not give compliance or IT a defensible trail. |

Result: partners know the risk; IT and ops cannot deploy something the firm can stand behind.

---

## 3. What good looks like

- **Approved UX** — ChatGPT-class experience inside a firm-controlled environment, not a shadow browser tab.
- **Citations** — Answers grounded in firm documents (policies, precedents, templates), not generic web training data.
- **Audit logs** — Who asked what, when; exportable for compliance and incident review.
- **AU-hosted → on-prem path** — Start on Australian infrastructure; migrate to on-prem open-source models on firm hardware without rebuilding the product.

---

## 4. Who this is for

Australian mid-tier **law, accounting, and advisory** firms with **50–250 staff** who need sovereign AI without enterprise Big 4 budgets or US-default inference.
