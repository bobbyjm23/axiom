## 1. Background

1.1 The Founder is the sole shareholder of the Company and does not currently ordinarily reside in Australia.

1.2 The Corporations Act 2001 (Cth), s201A requires that at least one director of the Company ordinarily resides in Australia.

1.3 The Resident Director has agreed to be appointed as a director of the Company to satisfy this requirement, on the terms set out in this Agreement.

1.4 The Resident Director's role is primarily a **compliance and governance seat**. Day-to-day operational decisions remain with the Founder as sole shareholder and [optional: non-resident director].

---

## 2. Appointment

2.1 The Company appoints the Resident Director as a director of the Company with effect from **[APPOINTMENT_DATE]**.

2.2 The Resident Director accepts the appointment and consents to act as director, subject to this Agreement.

2.3 The Founder, as sole shareholder, authorises this appointment by separate shareholder resolution.

---

## 3. Scope of role

3.1 The Resident Director's responsibilities are limited to:

(a) satisfying the Australian residency requirement under s201A Corporations Act 2001;

(b) receiving and forwarding ASIC, ATO, and other regulatory correspondence;

(c) signing documents where an Australian-resident director signature is required by law or third parties;

(d) attending [quarterly / as reasonably required] board meetings (which may be held by video conference);

(e) being kept informed of the Company's financial position and material matters.

3.2 The Resident Director is **not** responsible for:

(a) day-to-day management or operations of the Company;

(b) product development, sales, or customer delivery;

(c) hiring or firing employees (except as required by law);

(d) entering commercial contracts on behalf of the Company without the Founder's prior written approval.

3.3 All operational decisions require the Founder's prior written approval unless required by law.

## 5. Indemnity

5.1 The Company and the Founder jointly and severally indemnify the Resident Director against any liability, cost, or expense (including reasonable legal fees) incurred by the Resident Director:

(a) in performing the role described in Section 3, provided the Resident Director acted in good faith and in accordance with this Agreement and the Founder's written directions; and

(b) arising from any claim by a third party relating to the Resident Director's actions taken at the Founder's written direction.

5.2 The indemnity in clause 5.1 does **not** apply to liability arising from:

(a) the Resident Director's fraud, wilful misconduct, or gross negligence;

(b) the Resident Director's breach of director duties under the Corporations Act 2001;

(c) actions taken by the Resident Director without the Founder's prior written approval (where approval was required).

5.3 The Company will use reasonable efforts to obtain directors and officers (D&O) insurance when commercially practicable and notify the Resident Director when coverage is obtained.

---

## 6. Information and cooperation

6.1 The Founder will:

(a) keep the Resident Director informed of the Company's financial position, including providing [monthly/quarterly] management accounts;

(b) notify the Resident Director promptly of any material litigation, regulatory inquiry, or solvency concern;

(c) not direct the Resident Director to take any action that would breach the Corporations Act 2001 or cause the Resident Director to breach their director duties.

6.2 The Resident Director may request information reasonably necessary to fulfil director duties. The Founder will respond within [5] business days.

---

## 7. Resignation and replacement

7.1 The Resident Director may resign by giving **[30]** days' written notice to the Company and the Founder.

7.2 The Founder may remove the Resident Director by shareholder resolution, provided a replacement resident director is appointed **within 28 days** to satisfy s201A.

7.3 If the Resident Director resigns or is removed, the Founder must:

(a) appoint a replacement resident director within 28 days;

(b) notify ASIC of the change within 28 days; and

(c) ensure the Resident Director is released from any ongoing obligations (subject to surviving indemnity and director duties).

## 8. Confidentiality

8.1 The Resident Director must keep confidential all non-public information about the Company, its customers, and its business.

8.2 This obligation survives termination of this Agreement for **three (3) years**.

---

## 9. Term

9.1 This Agreement commences on **[APPOINTMENT_DATE]** and continues until terminated by:

(a) resignation under clause 7.1;

(b) removal under clause 7.2;

(c) mutual written agreement; or

(d) winding up of the Company.
