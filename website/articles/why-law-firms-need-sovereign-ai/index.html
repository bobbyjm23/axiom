The confidentiality problem is already here

Shadow AI isn't a future risk — it's current practice. Legal practitioners owe duties of confidentiality under the Legal Profession Uniform Law and uniform Rules. Pasting client instructions, draft advice, or discovery material into public AI services creates:

  • Potential waiver of legal professional privilege
  • Privacy Act exposure where personal information leaves your control
  • No audit trail for regulators or insurers
  • Reputational damage if a breach becomes public

Why Copilot alone isn't enough

Microsoft Copilot is the default board-level answer — familiar, bundled, fast to procure. But for firms handling confidential and legally privileged material, Copilot routes inference through US-controlled infrastructure at roughly $45–50 per user per month. At 75 lawyers that's $40k+ per year in recurring OpEx with no CapEx alternative and limited air-gap path.

Our positioning: Land with a hosted pilot in weeks, prove adoption with the same desktop UX lawyers already know, then convert to customer-owned on-premises when the partnership is ready — without rebuilding.
